2. The Candidate's Position

Adnan

We take a centrist position on gun control policy. We recognize there are issues concerning safety in schools and other public venues but maintain that the right to bear arms is a fundamental right and can be integrated into a safe American society. The following are the central tenets of our stance:

  • The right to obtain and store ammunition only with registration
  • Regulate access to hunting grounds and shooting ranges to individuals of a certain age (21) and registration requirement.
  • Implement federal licensing of guns to prevent illegal/irresponsible gun ownership and facilitate the process of confiscating guns when registration/ID requirements have not been met.
  • Implement features such as child safety feature locks and gun safety tests.
  • Federal funding for prosecution in gun crime lawsuits.
  • We will recognize the right of the NRA to fund and support candidates for office.
  • Technical innovation needed to fix core gun issues

7. History of Legislation

Sarah
  • National Firearms Act of 1934
    • Taxes the manufacture and transfer of, and mandates the registration of Title II weapons such as machine guns, short-barreled rifles and shotguns, heavy weapons, explosive ordnance, suppressors, and disguised or improvised firearms.
  • Gun Control Act of 1968
    • Regulates the firearms industry and firearms owners. It primarily focuses on regulating interstate commerce in firearms by generally prohibiting interstate firearms transfers except among licensed manufacturers, dealers and importers.
  • Omnibus Crime Control and Safe Streets Act of 1968
    • Prohibited interstate trade in handguns, increased the minimum age to 21 for buying handguns.
  • Firearm Owners Protection Act 1986
    • Revised and partially repealed the Gun Control Act of 1968. Prohibited the sale to civilians of automatic firearms manufactured after the date of the law's passage. Required ATF approval of transfers of automatic firearms.
  • Undetectable Firearms Act of 1988
    • Effectively criminalizes, with a few exceptions, the manufacture, importation, sale, shipment, delivery, possession, transfer, or receipt of firearms with less than 3.7 oz of metal content.
  • Gun-Free School Zones Act 1990
    • Prohibits unauthorized individuals from knowingly possessing a firearm at a place that the individual knows, or has reasonable cause to believe, is a school zone.

1. About the Issue

Amay

Protecting the environment has become more important to the Americans over the years. With the publication of Silent Spring by Rachel Carson, the public became aware of the impact of the pesticide DDT. As a result, this compound was banned, and eventually the Environmental Protection Agency was created by President Nixon. Since then, the issues around environmental protection and regulation have morphed from dealing with pure science into ones which need to balance the competing needs of keeping people healthy while allowing economic development and prosperity. The key issues facing people today include: (i) Global Warming/Climate Change, (ii) Reduction in harmful pollutants in the air and water, (iii) Managing wildlife to ensure biodiversity. Climate change refers to the impact of rising levels of greenhouse gases (gases that cause the temperature of the atmosphere is rise) in the atmosphere. With increased concentrations of these gases, the overall temperature in the atmosphere increases, and is causing changes in weather patterns and temperatures. While most people think of these changes as causing increased temperatures, they actually are causing increased extreme events, including colder temperatures in some locations and higher temperature in others. Keeping clean air and water for people and animals is another goal of environmental regulation and politics. However, it is not just as simple as stopping pollution. Economic development of a nation causes industries to release waste materials from factories. How much of each substance and how it can be released is the subject of environmental regulation. Sometimes, the regulations cause industries to close down, or force them to switch raw materials. For example, restrictions introduced by President Obama under the clean air act, are forcing Power Plants to consider switching from coal to natural gas. Finally, managing wildlife is another environmental issue. While humans are the largest species on Earth, there are millions of other species that call this plant home. However, many of them live in areas that humans want to develop for their own economic needs. How to balance the needs of the competing interests of human economic development and protection of these species is the role of environmental policy. For example, reintroducing wolves into Yellowstone has enabled the ecosystem to become rebalanced. However at the same time, wolves have been known to attack and kill cattle being raised by ranchers in the area. How to protect important wild species while also protecting the economic rights of people in the area is a difficult balancing act that politicians need to deal with.

2. The Candidate's Position

Brianna

Protecting the environment is a necessary goal that this administration strongly believes in accomplishing and supporting. Full government regulation is the most apparent option to change the worsening state of the environment, through business friendly regulations that still support free trade options. This in turn, will ensure that businesses are not hurt by regulations and efforts to lower the global temperature through the limiting of climate-altering pollution. The Paris Climate Agreement, while essential to combat climate change through the lowering of greenhouse gas emissions, ultimately does not fully embody the vision of this administration. This is why we support the renegotiation of the agreement, to include a carbon tax system, a cost-effective way to decrease greenhouse gas emissions. The implementation of a carbon tax system will serve to increase support for renewable energy sources, such as solar panels or wind turbines, that do not emit carbon dioxide. Giving block grants to the states for environmental funding will allow for them to allocate the funds as they desire, to support issues more pressing in concern to them individually.

“As the pioneer of environmentalism a century ago, the Republican Party reaffirms the moral obligation to be good stewards of the God-given natural beauty and resources of our country.” -Republican 2016 Platform

This emphasizes the beliefs of this administration in regards to protecting the natural environment through the implementation of a carbon tax system and state funding. The Republican Party ultimately aims to preserve the environment, and does not deny climate change is an increasing issue of international concern.

“We firmly believe environmental problems are best solved by giving incentives for human ingenuity and the development of new technologies, not through top-down, command-and-control regulations that stifle economic growth and cost thousands of jobs.” -Republican 2016 Platform

This illustrates how the Republican Party fully supports giving block grants to the states to use their discretion to decide which environmental matters most urgently need support and funding. Categorical grants are not the solution, and only serve to force the states into coercion to replicate the federal government’s agenda. New technology, such as wind turbines are a positive and clean source of energy, as opposed to fuels that emit carbon dioxide, increasing greenhouse gas emissions.
